## 1. Talas Akademija (Mario Vrečo — proof it works in our region)

Site funnel: hero "Nauči edit i radi od kuće" → 886-member counter → 4 persona cards →
3-step how-it-works → **video testimonials with full names** (Uroš Cekić, Teodora Milovanović…)
with money numbers ("750€ za par meseci") → course catalog → mentor bios → member-wins feed →
pricing with countdown (birthday promo $59 locked, standard $70) → FAQ.

Ad evolution (the lesson):
- 2025 ads: emoji feature lists + Black Friday countdowns → weak-format era.
- 2026 ads (current, active): **long-form pain-story copy**, no emojis:
  - Analogy hook: "Učenje video editinga online je kao da sastavljaš Ikea sto, bez uputstva."
  - Concrete buyer economics: "plaćaju po klipu… jeftinije da plate tebe 50e po videu nego da zaposle nekog za stalno."
  - The enemy = YouTube-alone: "Većina krene sama, preko YouTube tutorijala, pa zaglavi posle dve nedelje.
    Nema redosleda, nema feedbacka, nema prvog klijenta."
  - Then the mechanism: "Zato smo napravili Talas Akademiju: kursevi korak po korak, mentori…"
- Dedicated **testimonial ad** variant ("Evo šta kažu naši članovi…").
- **Win-back ad** to churned members ("Vrati se. Akademija više nije ista" — lists what's new since they left).
- **$1 tripwire feeder**: skool.com/nauciedit — "Nauči video editovanje za 7 dana, $1 jednokratno, 20 lekcija."
- Loyalty pricing: "što si duže član, cena ti se smanjuje" (from month 4: $50).

## 2. Top Skool communities — what ALL the $100k+ MRR winners do

1. **Founder-number headline, not testimonials** (7/8): "zero to over $1 million per year" (Herk),
   "$100K/month AI business" (Jack), "$300K/month AI tech stack" (Goldie), "$40k/m" (Chase).
2. **Member-count price ladder** (6/8): "price rises at N members" — self-verifying scarcity,
   never expires. Imperio: "$59 de por vida → $87 at 3k members" while AT 3,000.
3. **"The exact [asset] behind my $X"** framing — sell a copy of a working machine, not education.
4. **Aggregate win counters** beat individual testimonials: "290 documented wins" (Goldie),
   "551+ wins, $600K+ member revenue" (GenHQ), "+1.600 resultados reales" (Imperio, cash prizes for win posts).
5. **Guarantee tied to a deliverable**: "Use 1 workflow — no time saved, refund" (Goldie);
   build your first agent or refund (Imperio). Guarantee doubles as activation device.
6. Claude-specific players sell **Claude → income**, never "learn Claude"
   (CCC: "#1 Community for learning Claude Code and how to make money").
   WARNING: Claude Code Club anchors AGAINST our price: "once we hit 7,400 members new members pay $69/month —
   lock in $9." Our $69 must out-proof them, we can't out-price them.

## 3. Prompt-product sellers — the $17 Vault playbook

- Universal: big item count in headline · slashed anchor + "one-time, lifetime, no subscription" ·
  value stack of 8–11 named line items with $ values · "future updates included free" ·
  Notion/Airtable delivery · self-reported stars/user counts.
- Best free-objection lines in market (God of Prompt):
  "Most free prompts online are generic, untested, and produce output that screams 'AI wrote this.'"
  "You don't need 100,000 prompts. You need the 200 prompts that actually move your work."
  → At 550 curated items we can own curation-over-volume MORE honestly than a 30k-prompt seller.
- Techpresso is running Meta ads RIGHT NOW for "1000 prompts rebuilt from scratch for how Claude
  actually works — XML tags, artifacts, extended thinking" → validates our exact positioning;
  also their winning ad formats: "I asked Claude to build X. 90 seconds later…" + "The exact prompt is inside."
- Miles Stutz ad validates the $17 tripwire→high-ticket ladder in-market ("I sell a $17 product…
  a chunk of those buyers upgrade themselves").
- NOBODY in the prompt market upsells into a paid community → our differentiator, but unvalidated;
  bridge = "prompts go stale; membership is where they stay current + get implemented."
- Open proof lane nobody uses: **before/after output** (generic prompt vs vault recipe).
- Must have an answer for "Anthropic's prompt library is free" — nobody has written one.

## 4. Proof strategy (NO fabricated testimonials — illegal under FTC fake-reviews rule + Meta ad account bans)

Ranked by credibility-per-effort:
1. Real Skool post screenshots (already have win1–win5 images; mine more once cookies refreshed).
2. Aggregate counters that self-verify: members (1,200+), recipes shipped (550+), weekly call streak.
3. Named member quotes WITH PERMISSION — run a testimonial-ask post; specific questions produce
   quotable answers ("what did you build?", "what did it replace?", "time saved per week?").
4. Founder specificity: what Marko built/earns with Claude — the winners' #1 proof format.
5. A documented-wins counter fed by a #wins channel (start now; cite the number once >50).
